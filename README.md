# HEPro AI Business Analytics Fellowship (Jan 2026-June 2026)

### HR-Analytics-Dashboard-ZenithTech
**An interactive Power BI dashboard for ZenithTech to visualize key HR metrics, track attrition, and drive data-driven workforce planning

---
#### Project Overview
This project involves the creation of a comprehensive HR Analytics Dashboard for ZenithTech Solutions, a mid-sized technology firm. The primary goal is to monitor workforce stability, analyze attrition drivers, and provide data-backed recommendations to improve employee retention and engagement.  By auditing 1,470 employee records, the dashboard serves as an "Organizational Health Monitor," enabling leadership to transition from reactive practices to proactive talent management

#### Key Objectives
- Develop an interactive Power BI dashboard to track real-time HR KPIs.  
- Identify patterns and drivers behind the 23.27% attrition rate.  
- Analyze the correlation between salary segmentation and employee turnover.  
- Examine workforce demographics across age, gender, and education.  
- Provide strategic retention recommendations based on analytical findings.

---
#### Dataset Description
** The analysis is based on a structured dataset containing 1,470 records with various categorical and numerical variables:  

- Identification: EmployeeID.  
- Demographics: Age, Gender, Education Field.  
- Organizational: Department, Job Role, Years at Company (Tenure).  
- Compensation: Monthly Income.  
- Target Variable: Attrition (Yes/No).

---
#### Technical Implementation (DAX & Data Engineering)
** Calculated columns and measures were developed to enhance analytical depth:  

- Age Groups: Segmented into Early Career (16-30), Mid Career (31-45), and Senior (46+).  
- Salary Ranges: Grouped into 0-5K, 5-10K, and 10K+ brackets.  
- Key Measures: Total Attrition, Attrition Rate, Average Age, Average Salary, and Average Tenure.

---
#### Key Findings & Insights
- **High-Risk Roles:** Sales Executives (68 exits) and Research Scientists (63 exits) show the highest attrition volume.  
- **Financial Drivers:** Employees in the 0-5K salary range contribute the most to turnover (188 exits), suggesting compensation dissatisfaction at junior levels.  
- **Demographic Trends:** The 31-45 age group has the highest attrition (127 exits), and male employees account for 61% of total exits.  
- **Tenure Risk:** Higher exit frequency occurs within the first two years of employment, indicating potential onboarding gaps.

---
#### Recommendations
- **Retention:** Conduct stay interviews for high-risk roles and implement 90-day structured onboarding.  
- **Engagement:** Offer mid-career growth pathways and leadership development programs.  
- **Compensation:** Perform salary benchmarking and review structures for the 0-5K bracket
